License Plate Retention Policies by State

This table summarizes whether license plates stay with the vehicle or the seller upon sale or transfer, based on each state's DMV policy.

California Plate stays with vehicle High The license plate stays with the vehicle when sold, except in the case of disabled person, special interest, or personalized license plates which must be removed by the seller. Most vehicles have sequentially issued “standard” license plates that remain with the vehicle when ownership is transferred. Disabled person, special interest, or personalized license plates belong to the plate owner, not the vehicle. https://www.dmv.ca.gov/portal/how-to-change-vehicle-ownership-htvr-32/
Florida Plate stays with seller High In Florida, the license plate stays with the seller when a vehicle is sold. The seller has the option to transfer the plate to a new or replacement vehicle. However, under certain circumstances, the seller must surrender the tag to a motor vehicle service center. In a sale, license plates stay with seller not the vehicle. When motor vehicles are sold, the seller must remove the license plate from the vehicle and may then transfer the plate to a new or replacement vehicle.
